## Purpose

This prompt is designed to help the AI reason from first principles about renewable interconnection and infrastructure deployment workflows.

The goal is NOT to replicate an existing company.
The goal is to understand the structural bottlenecks in renewable energy deployment and redesign the workflow assuming AI-native capabilities.

This document should be used as a reasoning seed for system design, not as marketing copy.

---

## Context

Renewable energy developers in California must navigate a complex interconnection and deployment lifecycle:

1. Prospecting substations and transmission capacity
2. Estimating upgrade cost risk
3. Submitting interconnection requests
4. Waiting for cluster study results
5. Discovering upgrade obligations months later
6. Committing capital under uncertainty

The process is:

- Slow
- Capital-intensive
- Opaque
- Iterative but high-cost
- Dependent on consultants and delayed ISO feedback

Developers often:
- Wait months for feasibility clarity
- Discover fatal constraints late
- Waste deposits and development capital
- Lose project windows
- Miss competitive advantage

The bottleneck is not lack of raw data.
The bottleneck is the inability to reason across grid constraints, zoning complexity, land availability, and risk in real time.

---

## Core Problem Statement

Renewable infrastructure deployment suffers from delayed feasibility intelligence.

Developers cannot:
- Predict upgrade cost exposure early
- Run rapid what-if siting scenarios
- Evaluate multi-factor tradeoffs instantly
- Understand probabilistic risk before committing capital

This creates capital inefficiency and structural friction.
